FEES AND ACCOMODATION

For Delegates of Indian Origin:

Delegate or Press Registration Fees: INR 1,200 each, which will include the delegate kit, entry to all three social events, and dinner on all three nights.
Accommodation Fees : INR 800 each, which will include breakfast+lunch (dinner already provided from registration fees) and accommodation of three days and nights.

For Delegates of Foreign Origin:

Delegate or Press Registration Fees: USD 50 each, which will include the delegate kit, entry to all three social events, and dinner on all three nights.
Accommodation Fees : USD 25 each, which will include breakfast+lunch (dinner already provided from registration fees) and air conditioned accommodation of three days and nights.
Scholarship opportunities for students of foreign origin are available and can be found in the ‘SCHOLARSHIP OPPORTUNITIES’ section in the ‘Information’ tab of our website.

SCHOLARSHIP OPPORTUNITIES

Scholarship opportunities are only for students of foreign origin and not for Indian citizens.
The KIIT International MUN Scholarship will be awarded to deserving candidates based on their MUN experience and answers to a set of questions. To apply, send in your MUN experience in a .docx file to delegateaffairs@kiitmun.org and if you clear Round 1 you shall be sent the Round 2 question set within the next two days of us receiving your email. Mention your name and nationality in the email.
The Scholarship amounts to :
  • Full withdrawal of Registration fees amounting to 50 USD.
  • Full withdrawal of Accommodation Fees amounting to 25 USD.
  • Coverage of food expenses during the stay.
  • Airport/ Station drop & pick up in Bhubanaeswar.

TRAVEL PLANS

Bhubaneswar is well connected to all parts of the country through a good network of transport. Flights, railways and buses ensure hassle free travel for the international and out station delegates.

FOR INTERNATIONAL DELEGATES:

Our team is ready to provide travel assistance and well believing in the Sanskrit phase “ Atithi Devo Bhava”, which means, a guest to our country is equivalent to god; you can get in touch with the Secretary General directly at secretarygeneral@kiitmun.org or call on 0091-7855024743. Here is a quick guide:
A: From Your Country’s to India :
There are no direct flights from other countries to the Bhubaneswar Biju Patnaik International Airport except very few SAARC countries. In which case we would encourage you to book your flight tickets to New Delhi (Indira Gandhi International Airport) or to the city of Kolkata (Netaji Subash Chandra Bose International Airport)
B: From NEW DELHI/KOLKATA to BHUBANESWAR:
Indira Gandhi International Airport, New Delhi and Netaji Subash Chandra Bose International Airport, Kolkata are both well connected to Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar through various low cost domestic airlines such as Spice Jet, Go Air and Indigo airlines and other national carriers like Air India, Jet Airways, etc. You can log onto www.makemytrip.com for a quick view of your options!
It is also well connected through comfortable superfast trains namely the Rajdhani Express from New Delhi and East Coast Express, Shatabdi Express, Dhauli Express, Puri-Howrah Express, Jagannath Express, Garibrath, etc from Kolkata. There is a reserved International Tourist quota for immediate reservation of tickets for the international passengers travelling to any part of India. You can log on to www.irctc.in for booking, assistance and queries regarding travel by rail. The International Tourist Bureaus are located at Kolkata (Howrah) and New Delhi railway stations which function from 08:00hrs to 20:00hrs from Monday to Saturday and on Sunday from 08:00hrs to 14:00hrs. These provide assistance to the foreign tourists regarding enquiries, booking and reservation. There are also Volvo Bus Services from Kolkata called the Royal Cruiser Bus Service, tickets for which and assistance can be obtained from www.redbus.in
Airport and/or station pick up will be arranged once you send us your travel itinerary.

FOR NATIONAL DELEGATES:

Delegates travelling from cities like Kolkata, Delhi, Mumbai, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Chennai, Ahmadabad to Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar by availing any of the following domestic flights: Air India, Go Air, Jet Airways, Indigo Airlines.
Railway Facilities can also be availed to reach Bhubaneswar. Trains from Hyderabad include Bhubaneswar-Pune Express, Falaknuma Express and Yashwantpur Express. Coromandel Express connects Chennai to Bhubaneswar. Gitanjali Express and Duronto Express connect Mumbai and Bangalore to Bhubaneswar respectively. Numerous trains from Kolkata exist including the Shatabdi Express, Jaganath Express, etc.
Reaching KIIT University after arriving in Bhubaneswar is easy with KIIT being a local landmark, easily connected by city buses and local for-hire autos. Radio Taxi facilities are also available.


VISA INFORMATION

All foreign nationals entering India are required to possess a valid international travel document in the form of a national passport with a valid visa obtained from an Indian Mission or Post abroad.
The duly signed physical copy of the application form completed in all respect and submitted successfully, is to be submitted at the concerned Indian Visa Application Center (IVAC) or directly to Indian Mission/ Post, on the scheduled date of interview along with the requisite supporting documents. The instructions for filling the form and scheduling the appointment can be seen at Instructions for Online Visa Application. Important technical information for filling online Indian visa application can be referred at Technical Instructions.
The applicants are also requested to visit website of the Indian Mission concerned for detailed information about Indian visa. You can search Google for “ Indian Embassy _ Your County Name_ “
The e-Tourist Visa (eTV) functionality is available for following nationals : Finland, Japan, Luxembourg, New Zealand, Singapore, Cambodia, Vietnam, Philippines, Laos ,Myanmar, and Indonesia.
